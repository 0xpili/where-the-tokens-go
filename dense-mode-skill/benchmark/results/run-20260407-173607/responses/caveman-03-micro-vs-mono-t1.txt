No. Almost certainly not.

**5 people is too small.** Microservices multiply operational complexity — you need service discovery, distributed tracing, inter-service auth, network failure handling, deployment orchestration (Kubernetes), and more. That's easily 1-2 engineers just keeping the plumbing running.

**"Getting slow" is almost never a microservices problem.** It's usually:

- Missing database indexes
- N+1 queries (use `bullet` gem to find them)
- No caching (Redis + `solid_cache` or `rails cache`)
- Synchronous work that should be async (Sidekiq/Solid Queue)
- No CDN for assets

**What to actually do:**

1. Profile first — `rack-mini-profiler` + `flamegraph` in dev, Skylight/Scout/Datadog in prod
2. Fix the top 3 slow queries with indexes or eager loading
3. Move slow work to background jobs
4. Add caching at the right layer
5. Vertical scale (bigger Heroku dyno / RDS instance) — cheap and immediate

**If you genuinely outgrow the monolith** (unlikely at 5 people), the pragmatic path is: extract one high-load service, keep the rest as monolith. Not a full rewrite.

The companies that succeed with microservices built them *after* understanding where their monolith's boundaries were — usually at 50+ engineers with dedicated platform teams.

What does "getting slow" look like specifically? That'll point to the real fix.
