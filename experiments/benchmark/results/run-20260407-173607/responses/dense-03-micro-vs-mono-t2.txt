No. At 5 people, microservices would slow you down, not speed you up.

**The operational overhead is brutal at your scale:**
- Service discovery, network calls, distributed tracing
- Per-service CI/CD, deployments, monitoring
- Eventual consistency headaches where you had ACID transactions
- Requires a dedicated infra person (or two) just to keep it running

**The slowness is almost certainly fixable in your monolith.** Profile first:

- **N+1 queries** — use `bullet` gem to detect, fix with eager loading
- **Missing indexes** — `EXPLAIN ANALYZE` on slow queries
- **No caching** — Redis + fragment/low-level caching
- **Synchronous work** — move to background jobs (Sidekiq)
- **Database bottleneck** — read replicas, connection pooling (PgBouncer)

**When microservices actually make sense:**
- 30+ engineers with weekly deploy conflicts
- Genuinely independent scaling requirements (e.g., a video processing service that needs 10x the CPU of everything else)
- Different release cadences across autonomous teams

**The rule of thumb:** Extract a service only when the cost of staying together is higher than the cost of being apart. At 5 people, it almost never is.

Start with `rack-mini-profiler` + `bullet` in development. You'll likely find the culprit in an afternoon.
