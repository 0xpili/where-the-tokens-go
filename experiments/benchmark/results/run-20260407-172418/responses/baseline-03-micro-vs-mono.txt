For a 5-person team: almost certainly no. Here's the honest breakdown.

**Why your monolith is probably slow (and fixable without microservices):**
- Missing database indexes
- N+1 queries (Bullet gem will find them)
- No caching (Redis + fragment/Russian doll caching)
- Unoptimized background jobs (Sidekiq tuning)
- No CDN or asset pipeline issues

Fix these first. They're responsible for 80%+ of Rails slowness at your scale.

**The microservices cost you'd be taking on:**
- Network latency between services (often *worse* than monolith DB calls)
- Distributed transactions — you lose ACID, gain coordination hell
- Separate deploy pipelines, monitoring, logging per service
- 5 engineers maintaining N services instead of building product
- Eventual consistency bugs that are very hard to debug

**What actually makes sense at your scale:**
1. Profile first (`rack-mini-profiler`, `bullet`, slow query logs)
2. Add read replicas if DB is the bottleneck
3. Extract only truly separate *workloads* (e.g., image processing to a worker, not a "service")
4. Consider a modular monolith (Rails engines or clear bounded contexts) if coupling is the real problem

**The honest rule of thumb:** Microservices solve *organizational* scaling problems (50+ engineers stepping on each other) more than *technical* ones. At 5 people, the overhead will slow you down more than your monolith ever will.

What's actually slow? Queries, pages, background jobs? That would point to a much more targeted fix.
